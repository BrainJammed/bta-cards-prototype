Employee benefits and expenses

Some expenses and benefits are eligible for an exemption, meaning you won't have to pay tax on them.

Find out about exemptions

You must pay tax on any expenses and benefits not covered by a dispensation, and report them to HMRC.

Find out the tax rules for each type of expense or benefit.

How to report and pay tax

Once a year (P11D)

If you only pay small or irregular benefits and expenses, you can make a Payment Settlement Agreement (PSA) with HMRC.

Otherwise at the end of the tax year you need to submit:

  • an electronic P11D or paper P11D for each employee that received expenses or benefits
  • a P11D(b) for your company

You must also complete form P46(Car) if you start or stop providing an employee or director with a car for their private use.

Regularly through your payroll

You can report benefits and expenses and pay tax on them regularly throughout the year through your payroll. You'll need to use HMRC's new Payroll Benefits in Kind service.

You'll still need to submit a P11D(b) for your company. But you won't need to submit P11Ds for individual employees.
