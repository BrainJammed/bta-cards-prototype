Register for Corporation Tax, add Corporation Tax to your account or tell us you are no longer trading

Register for Corporation Tax with HMRC

When you have registered your company (opens in new tab) with Companies House, you must register for Corporation Tax with HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) within 3 months of being active.

Being active for Corporation Tax purposes includes:

  • buying
  • selling
  • advertising
  • renting a property
  • employing someone
  • similar trading activity

Check if you’re unsure what counts as being active (opens in new tab).

You must also register your company for Corporation Tax with HMRC if your company is registered as dormant, but is now active.

You may get a penalty if you register late.

What you need to tell HMRC when registering for Corporation Tax

You’ll need your company’s 10-digit Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR).

You usually get your UTR from HMRC within a few days of registering your company with Companies House.

Request your company’s UTR online if you did not get one after registering your company.

You’ll also need:

  • your company’s registration number (CRN)
  • the date you started to be active - your company’s first accounting period (opens in new tab) will start from this date
  • the date your annual accounts are made up to (the accounts preparation date)

What happens next

HMRC will tell you the deadline for paying Corporation Tax.

You must file a Company Tax Return (opens in new tab), even if you make a loss or have no Corporation Tax to pay.

Add Corporation Tax to your business tax account

Add Corporation Tax to your business tax account to:

  • file your Company Tax return online
  • make a payment online

Tell HMRC you are no longer trading

To stop paying Corporation Tax, you must close the limited company (opens in new tab).
