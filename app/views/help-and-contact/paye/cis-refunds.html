PAYE refunds

If HMRC owes you money for employer's PAYE, you can deduct the amount that you are owed from your next employer's PAYE payment to HMRC.

You do not need to tell HMRC.

If the refund is because you made a mistake in a payroll submission, you must correct the submission before you adjust your payment to HMRC.

Apply for a refund by post

You can also request a refund by post.

It takes several months for HMRC to process refund requests. It is faster to deduct the refund from your next employer's PAYE payment.

You will only get a refund if you do not owe money for other taxes.

Write to the following address, including details of the bank account you want the refund to go to:

National Insurance Contributions and Employers Office
HM Revenue and Customs
BX9 1BX
United Kingdom

CIS refunds

If you are a limited company that acts as a subcontractor under the Construction Industry Scheme (CIS) and your CIS account is in credit at the end of the year, you can request a repayment.

We aim to process repayments within 25 working days of receiving it, if we agree with your claim.

Do not call unless it has been more than 40 working days, as we may still be considering your claim.
