Get started with PAYE for employers

1. Payroll software

You can't pay your employees until you've installed payroll software.

Each time you pay an employee, the software will:

  • notify HMRC
  • calculate what you need to pay HMRC in tax and National Insurance

You can use either:

  • HMRC's Basic PAYE Tools (BPT) software (it's free)

  • commercial payroll software (there are some free packages)

2. Paying your employees

You must make a submission through your payroll software each time you pay an employee.

This is called a Full Payment Submission (FPS).

You must submit the FPS on or before the day you pay your employee, except when you can submit a late FPS.

If you don't pay any employees

If you don't pay any of your employees in a tax month, you still need to make a submission through your payroll software.

But instead of an FPS, you need to submit a 'nil' Employer Payment Summary (EPS) after the tax month has ended.

Your software will keep a record of each FPS and EPS you submit.

3. Paying HMRC

You need to use your accounts office reference when you pay HMRC.

Your accounts office reference is 13 characters, for example 123PA01234567. It's different to your employer's PAYE reference. You can find it on either:

  • the letter from HMRC when you first registered for PAYE
  • the 'Paying PAYE electronically' letter HMRC sends you at the start of each tax year (the 'P30B')

Make sure to use the correct accounts office reference if you manage more than one payroll scheme.
