Self Assessment: payment and penalties

When to pay your Self Assessment tax bill

The deadlines for paying are:

  • 31 January 2022 for your balancing payment. This is for tax year 2020 to 2021
  • 31 January 2022 for your first payment on account. This is for tax year 2021 to 2022
  • 31 July 2022 for your second payment on account. This is for tax year 2021 to 2022

You do not have to wait until 31 January or 31 July to pay. If you do, you might miss the deadline, depending on how you pay.

How to pay your Self Assessment tax bill

Make sure you pay HMRC by the deadline. You’ll be charged interest and may have to pay a penalty if your payment is late.

The time you need to allow depends on how you pay.

You can no longer pay at the Post Office.

Viewing your Self Assessment calculation

HMRC’s online tax return automatically works out how much you need to pay. You fill in your figures and once you have checked that everything is correct, you can view your calculation.

The calculation shows:

  • how your tax bill has been worked out
  • your income
  • your personal allowance
  • the tax due

  • any class 2 and class 4 National Insurance contributions (opens in new tab)

You can print a copy of the calculation for your records.

Penalties

You’ll have a penalty showing on your account if you:

  • sent in your tax return late
  • paid tax late
  • didn’t pay enough tax
  • the tax due

Appealing a penalty

If your business has been affected by coronavirus (COVID-19), you have an extra 3 months to appeal any penalty dated February 2020 or later.

You can appeal a penalty if you have a reasonable excuse, for example you had an unexpected stay in hospital. You must appeal within 30 days of the date the penalty was sent to you.

Your penalties and interest

You have no penalties or interest to display.
