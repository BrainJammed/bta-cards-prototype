Self Assessment: registering or stopping

Self Assessment tax returns

Self Assessment is a system HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) uses to collect Income Tax.

Income Tax is usually deducted automatically from wages, pensions and savings.

People and businesses with other income must report it by filing a Self Assessment tax return.

Self Assessment is used by:

  • the self-employed
  • sole traders
  • individuals such as company directors
  • partners in partnerships
  • trustees
  • other similar people

If you receive a tax return, or a notice to file, you must complete a return and submit it to HMRC.

Who must file a Self Assessment tax return (opens in new tab)

If you must file a tax return, you do so after the end of the tax year it applies to.

The Self Assessment tax year is 6 April to 5 April. For the tax year 6 April 2020 to 5 April 2021, you must file your return by 31 January 2022.

You need to register for Self Assessment if:

  • you must file a tax return for 2020 to 2021

and

  • you did not file one for the tax year 2019 to 2020

You should allow up to 20 working days if you have never filed an online tax return before.

To file your tax return online, you should:

  • be registered for Self Assessment
  • add Self Assessment to your tax account
  • complete and file your tax return by 31 January 2022
  • pay what you owe by 31 January 2022

If you do not register, file or pay on time, you may have to pay a penalty.

If you have not received a notice to file a Self Assessment tax return, you can check anonymously if you need to file a return (opens in new tab).

Class 2 National Insurance contributions

When you register for Self Assessment as self employed, you are also registering for Class 2 National Insurance contributions (opens in new tab).

Registering for Self Assessment

Your Self Assessment Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) is a 10 digit number. You can find it on:

  • your Welcome to Self Assessment letter (SA250)
  • tax returns you completed in previous years
  • other documents from HMRC

You can find your UTR (opens in new tab) if you do not know it.

If you are not self-employed

If you did not file a tax return last year and have to file one this year, use form SA1 to register for Self Assessment (opens in new tab).

If you are self-employed

If you did not file a tax return last year and have to file one this year, register for Self Assessment.

If you are a partner or partnership

You will need the Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) for the partnership and your own personal UTR if you already have one.

If it is a new partnership, the nominated partner will also have to register the partnership.

Find out more about registering as a partner or partnership (opens in new tab).

Stopping Self Assessment

You must tell HMRC if you do not need to file tax returns any more.

This could be because you are:

  • stopping self-employment
  • no longer trading as a sole trader
  • ending or leaving a business partnership

You will need to send final tax returns and tell employees that you are closing your business.

We will stop your Self Assessment and cancel your Class 2 National Insurance contributions.

Stopping your Class 2 National Insurance contributions may mean you lose certain benefits.

You can choose to stay registered for these contributions after stopping Self Assessment if you want to make voluntary payments. This will allow you to claim benefits like Tax-Free Childcare or Maternity Allowance.
