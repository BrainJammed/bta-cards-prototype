Help with your business tax account

Your business tax account

This is your business tax account. It lets you manage your HMRC business taxes, duties and schemes online.

You don’t have to own or run a business to have a business tax account.

To manage a tax, duty or scheme, you must add a tax to your business tax account.

If you do not know why you have a business tax account

You may not think of yourself as a business. However, even if you are not incorporated as a limited company, you may need a business tax account.

For example, Self Assessment is a business tax if you’re self-employed or a sole trader and you earn taxable income by:

  • renting property
  • selling goods and services
  • similar activity

You also need a business tax account when you:

  • set up a limited company or other organisation that pays Corporation Tax
  • operate PAYE because you employ people
  • charge VAT

How to manage your business tax account details

Keep a record of the Government Gateway user ID and password that you used to create your business tax account.

Your business tax account uses 2-stage verification. This means that every 7 days you'll need to verify your identity to sign in. We will ask you to enter an access code sent to another device or app.

Do not create another Government Gateway user ID and password. To view all your business taxes in one place you must use only one ID for all your online business tax services (opens in new tab) and sign into the business tax account with it.

The difference between your business tax account and personal tax account

Your personal tax account is for online management of your:

  • Income Tax and Self Assessment
  • PAYE
  • benefits
  • National Insurance
  • State Pension
  • other personal records and details

Read more about your personal tax account (opens in new tab)

Your business tax account is for online management of your:

  • Self Assessment
  • Making Tax Digital for Income Tax
  • VAT
  • Making Tax Digital for VAT
  • Corporation Tax
  • PAYE for employers
  • other business taxes, duties and schemes

Read the full list of taxes duties and schemes you can manage (opens in new tab)

Using your business tax account

Use your account to:

  • check your tax position for taxes that you’ve registered for
  • make returns and payments
  • add or remove a tax, duty or scheme
  • a team member access to a tax, duty or scheme
  • check secure messages from HMRC
  • add, view or change a tax agent
  • get help with your taxes
  • update your contact details
  • sign up for paperless notifications
  • change your Government Gateway password
